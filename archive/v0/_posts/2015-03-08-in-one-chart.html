There is a trend of articles claiming to explain things "in one chart". The trend makes sense: we're in a complex world and someone out there has at least made sense of a small slice of it. And look, all it takes is one chart.

But really, looking at each individual chart is too much effort. Imagine what you can learn from seeing all the "in one chart" charts in a single chart?

I kid; though, I'm more concerned about what I see as a reductionist trend, especially in a burgeoning field like data journalism. I'm concerned about the (deceitful) charts that are still to come that mirror data visualizations of years past:

Apple misrepresents it's marketshare.

"When we understand that slide, we'll have won the war"
--General Stanley McChrystal


This is all to say data literacy is a real need. For an increasingly used medium in todays news, public understanding of these new mediums should ideally increase in lock step.
