Methods for Point Tracking

For Graphs with Multiple, Non-Linear Lines

For every move of the mouse, search for the closest point among all points on the chart. With fewer lines displayed, performance is enhanced.

Under the hood

Areas of the chart are tied to the closest line for that area. When the mouse moves to a new area, the closest point on that area's line is computed. Note that now we only have to search the data points for one line, rather than points for all lines.

Under the hood

Areas of the chart are tied to the closest line for that area. The closest point for each area is precomputed and stored so that there are no on-the-fly, closest point searches. Smooth!
